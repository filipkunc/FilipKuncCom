I worked on a bunch of hobby projects with my brother years ago, mostly games. I wanted to revive some of them and keep them alive.

## Space Warrior

A 2D side-scrolling platformer my brother and I built around 2002-2003 in
Visual Studio. I was 16 at the time, just learning C++ and
[DirectDraw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DirectDraw), which was the easiest
way to push pixels around on a Pentium. A few years later I re-did the
renderer in OpenGL, and that OpenGL build is what Claude and I compiled to
WebAssembly with Emscripten. The game code is preserved almost verbatim;
only the platform layer (window, input, sprites, text) was replaced.

## Gentis

A third-person 3D game I never quite finished. Started around 2004-2005
and heavily rebuilt around 2007-2008. By then my C++ had improved and I
was getting comfortable with 3D math and graphics programming.

Even unfinished, it's where I picked up [Lua](https://www.lua.org/)
scripting, [OpenGL](https://www.opengl.org/), and a pile of
[NVIDIA](https://developer.nvidia.com/) SDKs.

I ported this one to WebGL 2 and WebAssembly by hand. The rendering had to
be rewritten from scratch. The original relied on the fixed-function
pipeline and other features that simply don't exist in modern GL.
